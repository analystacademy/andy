Your Privacy Choices

Privacy Certain U.S. state privacy laws — including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) — give you the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information for the purposes of cross-context behavioral advertising. This page explains your choices and how to exercise them.

For our full Privacy Policy, visit Privacy Policy. For all other privacy requests, email team@theanalystacademy.com with subject "Privacy Request."

What Does "Sale or Sharing" Mean?

Under the CCPA/CPRA, "sharing" includes disclosing your personal information to third-party advertising platforms — such as Meta, Google, and LinkedIn — through advertising pixels and cookies, even when no money changes hands. This enables those platforms to show you targeted ads based on your browsing behavior across different websites.

We use the following advertising and analytics tools on our site that may constitute "sharing" under California law:

  • Meta Pixel — Facebook/Instagram advertising
  • Google Ads — Google advertising and conversion tracking
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag — LinkedIn advertising and analytics
  • Google Analytics 4 — Site analytics (pseudonymous)
  • Hotjar — Experience analytics (pseudonymous)

How to Opt Out

Cookie & Advertising Preferences

You can manage your cookie and advertising preferences at any time using our cookie preference center. Choosing to opt out will disable advertising pixels and limit analytics tracking on your browser.

⚙️ Open Cookie Preferences — Manage your advertising and analytics consent choices.

Global Privacy Control (GPC)

We honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal. If your browser is configured to send a GPC signal, we will treat it as a valid opt-out of the sale and sharing of your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising — no additional action required.

To enable GPC, use a browser or extension that supports it, such as globalprivacycontrol.org.

Industry Opt-Out Tools

You can also use industry-wide opt-out tools:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) opt-out
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) opt-out
  • Google Ad Settings
  • Facebook Ad Preferences

Note: These tools are browser- and device-specific. If you use multiple browsers or devices, you may need to opt out on each one.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights beyond opt-out of sale/sharing, including the right to access, delete, or correct your personal information. To submit any privacy request:
