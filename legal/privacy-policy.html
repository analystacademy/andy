1. Who We Are

Analyst Academy LLC ("Analyst Academy," "we," "us," or "our") is an online education company headquartered in Kansas City, Missouri. We provide self-paced video courses, downloadable resources, and live team training focused on presentation and slide-building skills, serving 35,000+ students at leading organizations worldwide.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you use our website at theanalystacademy.com, our online courses, video lessons, live training programs, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

By accessing or using our Services, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you disagree, please discontinue use of our Services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Registration: Name, email address, password, job title, and company or organization name.
  • Course Enrollment: Selected courses, enrollment date, and payment details (processed by Stripe or PayPal — we do not store full card numbers).
  • Live Training Registration: Name, email, organization, number of attendees, and scheduled session details.
  • Communications: Messages you send us via email, contact forms, or support requests.
  • Email Subscriptions: Email address and preferences when you opt in to our newsletter or free email course.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, links clicked, time on page, referral URLs, browser type, operating system, IP address, and general location (country/region).
  • Course Activity Data: Video lesson views, lesson completions, course progress percentage, quiz results, and certificate issuance.
  • Device Information: Device type, screen resolution, and browser capabilities.
  • Cookies & Tracking: Session cookies, persistent cookies, pixel tags, and similar technologies (see Section 7).

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Payment confirmation and fraud signals from Stripe and PayPal.
  • Email engagement data (open rates, click rates) from Kit (formerly ConvertKit).
  • Aggregated analytics data from Google Analytics 4 and Hotjar.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • Provide and Deliver Services: Create and manage your account, enroll you in purchased courses, track your progress, issue completion certificates, and schedule live training sessions.
  • Process Payments: Charge for course purchases and live training, issue refunds, and detect fraud.
  • Customer Support: Respond to questions, resolve issues, and process refund requests.
  • Marketing & Communications: Send course updates, new course announcements, promotional offers, and our free email course. You may unsubscribe at any time.
  • Improve Our Services: Analyze how students engage with courses and our website to improve curriculum, UX, and content quality.
  • Legal Compliance: Meet legal obligations, enforce our Terms & Conditions, and protect our rights.
  • Safety & Security: Detect and prevent fraudulent activity, unauthorized account access, and abuse.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data under the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): Account creation, course enrollment, and payment processing.
  • Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Marketing to existing customers, analytics, and service improvement.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Marketing emails to new subscribers and non-essential cookies. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Tax records, fraud prevention, and other legal requirements.

5. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only in the following circumstances:

  • Service Providers (Subprocessors): Third-party vendors who process data on our behalf to operate our Services (see Section 6).
  • Business Transfers: If Analyst Academy is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your data may transfer as part of that transaction, subject to the same privacy protections.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose data when required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of Analyst Academy, our users, or the public.
  • With Your Consent: We will share data with third parties when you explicitly authorize it.

6. Third-Party Subprocessors

We use the following service providers, each of which processes data only as necessary for its described function:

  • Thinkific — Course platform and LMS. Processes: account data, course enrollment, progress, lesson activity, and certificate issuance. Privacy Policy
  • Stripe — Payment processing. Processes: billing name, address, and payment card data. Privacy Policy
  • PayPal — Payment processing. Processes: billing name, PayPal account details, and transaction records. Privacy Policy
  • Kit (formerly ConvertKit) — Email marketing. Processes: email address, name, subscription status, and email engagement data. Privacy Policy
  • Google Analytics 4 — Web analytics. Processes: anonymized usage data, device info, and anonymized IP address. Privacy Policy
  • Hotjar — Experience analytics (heatmaps, session recordings). Processes: mouse movements, clicks, scroll behavior (anonymized), and device info. Privacy Policy

7. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate and improve our Services:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for login sessions, course access, and security. These cannot be disabled without affecting core functionality.
  • Analytics Cookies: Google Analytics 4 uses cookies to measure website traffic and usage patterns (IP addresses are anonymized).
  • Experience Cookies: Hotjar uses cookies to record sessions and generate heatmaps for UX improvement purposes.
  • Marketing Cookies: May be used to track conversions from advertising campaigns.

You can control non-essential cookies through your browser settings or via our Privacy Choices page. Disabling cookies may impair some functionality.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our Services and fulfill the purposes described in this policy:

  • Account data: Retained while your account is active and for 3 years after closure, for legal and business purposes.
  • Course activity data: Retained for 5 years to support certificate verification and re-enrollment.
  • Payment records: Retained for 7 years to comply with tax and accounting requirements.
  • Marketing data: Retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Analytics data: Retained per default settings of Google Analytics 4 (up to 14 months) and Hotjar (up to 1 year).

9. Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including HTTPS/TLS encryption, access controls, and regular security reviews. Sensitive payment data is handled exclusively by PCI-DSS compliant processors (Stripe and PayPal) and is never stored on our servers.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach affecting your rights, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

10. International Transfers

Analyst Academy is based in the United States. If you are located in the EEA, UK, or other regions with data protection laws, your information will be transferred to and processed in the US. We rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and other appropriate safeguards for such transfers. Our subprocessors maintain their own cross-border transfer mechanisms as described in their respective privacy policies.

11. Your Rights — GDPR (EEA & UK)

If you are located in the EEA or UK, you have the following rights under the GDPR:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Restriction: Ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is consent-based.
  • Lodge a Complaint: File a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

To exercise these rights, contact us at team@theanalystacademy.com. We will respond within 30 days (or as required by applicable law).

12. Your Rights — CCPA (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants you the following rights:

  • Know: The categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, and the purposes for which it is used.
  • Delete: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Opt-Out of Sale: We do not sell personal information. No opt-out is needed.
  • Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.
  • Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information.

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information: We do not sell or share your personal information with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising. For more options, visit our Privacy Choices page.

To submit a CCPA request, email team@theanalystacademy.com with "CCPA Request" in the subject line. We will verify your identity before fulfilling the request.
